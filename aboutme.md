### My Robot Side
This is the section I was least willing to write, so I left it blank for years after creating this website. Describing myself formally has always felt awkward, and I don't have the flowery language to make it vivid or poetic. But here we are, despite the reluctance—because that is what life is about.

I currently work as a Senior Autonomous Vehicles Behavior Planning Engineer at NVIDIA. Before NVIDIA, I led the Trajectory Selection component at AutoX. Earlier, I worked on behavior planning for Waymo's trucking team and on model verification in MathWorks' Simulink Code Inspector team, helping ensure that models intended for safety-critical hardware could be deployed safely and efficiently.

I hold a Master's degree in Robotics from the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, where I conducted research on motion planning and task allocation for heterogeneous multi-robot systems under the supervision of Prof. Katia Sycara. During a summer break, I interned with Uber ATG's motion planning team, focusing on trajectory optimization. You can find details about my thesis work and related publications on this website.

I received my B.S. degree in Engineering Mechanics, with minors in both Computer Science and Mathematics, from University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) in 2018. I had the honor to work with Prof. James Allison, Prof. Randy Ewoldt, and Prof. Elif Ertekin on various topics like structural optimization, visco-elastic material design, and shape memory alloy simulation and design. I published two conference papers as the first author during my college years, which are also listed in the Research section of this website.

### My Human Side
When I'm not working on robotics and optimization, I indulge in artsy side projects like photography, pixel art, and playwriting. You can find them under the _Here and There_ tab.
